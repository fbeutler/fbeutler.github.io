The relative velocity effect as a potential systematic for Baryon Acoustic Oscillations

The relative velocity between cold dark matter and baryons has long been neglected because it considered a small effect. In recent years measurements have reached very high precision and systematic errors are a major concern for all cosmological observables. The Baryon Acoustic Oscillation (BAO) scale is now one of the most powerful tools of cosmology and is one of the core science drivers of most future larger scale structure projects.

The suppression of growth in baryon perturbations before decoupling is one source of relative velocity between baryons and cold dark matter. This effect is particularly interesting for BAO, since it acts on the same scale. I have studied this effect in a couple of publications (arXiv:1612.04720v3, arXiv:1506.03900v2).

Higher order clustering of galaxies in the Universe

The clustering of galaxies can be used to study properties of the Universe. A different amount of dark Energy in the Universe will lead to a different clustering pattern. Therefore by statistically analyzing the matter distribution in the Universe we can learn about the properties of dark energy. Similarly we can learn about the age of the Universe, the amount of dark matter, the properties of neutrinos, the nature of gravity etc.

A statistically optimal approach to study the clustering of galaxies is the calculation of the so called N-point functions. The lowest-order N-point function is a standard tool in such studies, but the inclusion of higher order N-point functions has been prooven difficult.

I am interested in developing efficient estimators for the galaxy bispectrum (Fourier-transform of the 3-point function) and use them for cosmological constraints. This project involves to come up with statistically optimal estimators and to find computational efficient algorithms to calculate these estimators. Ultimately this project will use the datasets like BOSS and eBOSS to constrain cosmological models.
